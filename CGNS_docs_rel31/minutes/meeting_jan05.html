  5. ISO status/discussion:

    1. Status from Cosner (e-mail):
      1. No realistic prospect of regaining Boeing funding, and thus Boeing participation, for this project. Authority to decide funding for ISO-STEP projects now rests with a Boeing org that is focused on CAD tools for mechanical/structural design. They have no interest in development of a fluid dynamics standard.
      2. ISO-STEP has decided to merge the draft CGNS-based fluids standard into the existing standard for finite element solid mechanics (AP 209), because there is a high degree of common content. Therefore, the standard will be discrete-element models for both fluids and solids. This effort is being led by Keith Hunten of Lockheed Martin, Fort Worth.
      3. The combined fluids-solids standard will be released in a new edition (update) of AP 209. A release date has not been established, but it is unlikely to be in 2005.
    2. Committee held brief discussion on compatibility of CGNS with the ISO version now that the ISO version may end up being radically different.
      1. Will CGNS still achieve a certain level of compliance?
      2. Does the committee need another "ISO representative" to keep track of the new developments?
      3. Decision for now to try to keep communication lines open. Rumsey to contact Keith Hunten of Lockheed Martin.

  8. HDF-5 status/discussion:

    1. Rumsey reported that Koziol (NCSA) e-mailed to say that they were planning to implement "creation order" tracking of objects in groups for the 1.8.0 release (due approximately in March 2005). He also continues to work on speedup for the large number (thousands) of zones (which is still significantly slower than ADF).
    2. Rumsey only received 1 response from users who tried the HDF5 version. The comments were:
      1. it is not backward compatible
      2. the HDF5 version of the test files were 2-3 times larger than the ADF files
    3. Discussion of these issues
      1. Backward compatibility should be easily overcome through converters between ADF and HDF5. These converters already exist. Software should be able to incorporate the converters.
      2. The issue of larger HDF5 files was noticed by other people as well. If only 1 zone, then the ADF and HDF5 files are usually of similar size. It is with many zones that they tend to be different. Rumsey will contact Koziol to make him aware of this issue.
      3. To summarize, there appears to be 2 issues regarding HDF5 that are "worse" than with ADF files: first is excessively slow I/O when there are many (thousands) of zones; second is larger file sizes, even for "standard" files.
    4. Discussion of switchover to HDF5
      1. Discussion about HDF5 two problems, and how they affect our switchover.
      2. Creation ordering in CGNS can probably be thrown away.
      3. Wedan will make change in CGNS HDF5 software so viewer can read (see) character strings in the HDF5 files.
      4. IL is looking at compression issues. Legensky plans to keep the committee informed about what they find.
      5. With new version of HDF5, links are a lot faster.
      6. Hauser currently has some parallel API calls, similar to partial read/writes just added by IL.
      7. The (unchanged) list of things to do/consider prior to HDF5 switchover is included below.
      8. Final decision of committee:
        1. Do not formally switchover to HDF5 yet. We need to resolve some of the performance and size issues first.
        2. V2.4beta will be modified by Wedan to include HDF5 as an option (at compile time). This beta release can then undergo further testing. We will re-evaluate formal switchover date in the future.
